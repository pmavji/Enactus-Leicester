About us

Are you looking to make a change in the world? Do you want to become the one who initiates the spark to thrive as a leader? Are you willing to go the extra mile?

Who We Are

Enactus is a worldwide non-profit organisation that focuses on the empowerment of the individual through entrepenurial action. Our aim is to help the disadvantaged communities to make change in the world, one step at a time.

Here at Enactus Leicester, we are a team of passionate and creative individuals that are shaping the future in our own way. Each of us is a collaborator at heart, and we never plan to stop!

What We Do

Three words. Recognise, Generate, Empower. Our team scouts local and international communities that are disadvantaged and create a plan to help them. We do this by putting together a team of like-minded students and form a project group focused to identify the problems and solve them.

Enactus is humbly sponsored by worldwide corporations and social enterprises that share our beliefs, allowing us to unlock our successful potential.

Operation Chandpur

"769 million people lack access to clean water across the world. 840,000 people die from water related disease each year."

What is your project about?

Operation Chandpur is a project made by entrepreneurial students of the university of Leicester that wants to help to flight the alarming problem of contaminated water in the world. The project emphasizes in build a water purification filter that can efficiently eliminate high levels of Arsenic from the water.

The project is based Bangladesh where the team identified one the highest levels of Arsenic in the world with more than 22 million drinking water unsanitary water. Moreover, the project identified that more than 74% of the population lives with less than $2 a day which limits them to acquire an adequate purification system.

The aim of the project is to build a cheap an efficient water system will be affordable to the local communities. The project emphasizes in two stages of development in order to purify the water. The First stage of the project is to develop a filter that uses a gravitational system in order to purify the water. Additionally, It uses locally sourced materials like sand and charcoal, and an Iron dust component to purify the Arsenic.

The second stage is designed as a simple water recollection system that uses recycled bottles to store large amounts of water. It can not only be used as storage but also as a rainwater collection system. The system can economically store up to 96 litres of water. A regular storage system of this kind could cost thousands of dollars.

What does your role as project leader involve?

My project leader functions at Operation Chandpur are to first set a clear plan of action to do over the year. Recognize possible weaknesses and strengths and what we have to do in order to get the best results. Following this, I have to identify the skills, interest and motivations of the group members in order to coordinate in the more adequate way their skills with the project objectives. In other words, get the best of each individual in order to get the best of the project.

What is your project about?

FreshStart is a project started in the summer of 2015 that aims to address two issues within Leicestershire – homelessness and food waste. In essence, our project has 2 overall objective. Firstly, to improve our participant’s quality of life by developing their employability skills to aid in future job employment and secondly, to utilise unused food received by our Project Partner (Action Homeless) to create healthy meals and food packs for its self-catered residents.

Our objective is to work with the same participants throughout the entire journey to develop both knowledge & skills (via workshops) and practical skills (via preparation of ready-meals and food packs).

We hope to work closely with our participants in future to aid them in job applications and in developing other skills which they need or want.

What does your role as project leader involve?

As Project Leader, the most important thing is deciding your leadership style. Day to day, we’re solely responsible for managing the project and providing direction for the project. We’re the bridge between project members and the executive committee, responsible for communicating with project partners and all relevant stakeholders. Project leaders plan meetings, complete administrative documents, and manage all schedules and tasks related to our specific project. In essence, we lead and are accountable for the success and failures of the team.

Why did you choose this project?

FreshStart gives you the opportunity to develop a variety of skills. Similar to all Enactus projects, all meetings are held as a team, allowing you to develop teamwork and your communication skills.

Specifically to FreshStart, you’ll get to opportunity to give presentations to a small group of participants. If public speaking is an obstacle for you, we’re the perfect place to start, as you’ll have a small audience to begin with. You’ll also get the opportunity to develop your problem solving skills! With FreshStart, obstacles are inherent with the project’s nature, but we believe in the power of synergy and together, we’ll find a solution!

Farmers Market

What is your project about?

The project aims to improve student nutrition and encourage students to support local businesses. Hosting 2-4 markets a year, the long-running Farmers’ Market project is also one of Enactus Leicester’s commercial project. Furthermore, for the past 12 months, the Farmers’ Market has initiated and supported the growth of another commercial project, now formally known as Revive smoothies.

What does your role as project leader involve?

Organisation and implementation of well-thought out event planning and preparation. As the project leader, I am the main point of contact between all stakeholders and team members within the project. Furthermore, I actively encourage my team to organise aspects of the market in preparation for the event itself.

Why did you choose this project?

I have always been brought up on the importance of a good diet and supporting local produce. As an entrepreneurial individual, I also enjoyed communicating with active businesses and providing a platform for students to experience something a bit different on campus.

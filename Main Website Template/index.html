About us

Are you looking to make a change in the world? Do you want to become the one who initiates the spark to thrive as a leader? Are you willing to go the extra mile?

Who We Are

Enactus is an international organization that connects student, academic and business leaders through entrepreneurial-based projects that empower people to transform opportunities into real, sustainable progress for themselves and their communities.

Here at Enactus Leicester, we are a team of passionate and creative individuals that are shaping the future in our own way. Each of us is a collaborator at heart, and we never plan to stop!

What We Do

Seeing Opportunity. Taking Action. Enabling Progress. Our teams identify market opportunities in both local and international communities and undertake in-depth needs and wants assessments to understand key barriers to improving livelihoods. We then form a project group of like-minded students who create and implement enterprises which transform lives in an economically, socially and environmentally sustainable way.

Enactus is sponsored by worldwide corporations that share our beliefs, allowing us to unlock our successful potential.

Toast Of The Town

"Combating food waste in the form of bread within Leicester."

What is your project about?

Toast of the town is our brand new social enterprise currently under development. The idea was generated from the Hackathon over summer 2016. The project involves turning surplus bread into alcoholic beverages for sale. This is an up-cycle strategy that combats food waste in the form of bread within Leicester.

At the current stage, we are focusing on the commercial aspect of the project, such as the production of the beverages, sales channels and marketing plans. As we want to set up a good foundation to later transform into a social enterprise, we are developing our strategy to employ beneficiaries into the model.

We are also trying to locate the best target group to fit into our vision and culture. At the moment, ex-offender and ex-serviceman are quite likely to be our potential beneficiary.

What does your role as project leader involve?

I have to set out the project plan and associated time line to make sure tasks can be done on time. Externally, I have to speak with our enterprising officer and make contacts with the businesses, like breweries and bakeries in Leicester.

Why did you choose this project?

This project interests me in many ways. First, I am a chemistry student and I think I could apply my knowledge into the brewery part. Second, I have the chance to expand my experience from a regular chemist, into an entrepreneur.

I am working in a start-up environment with a group of student from different background with hopes to strengthen my skills, such as communication, analytical thinking and time management.

Fresh Start

"Creating healthy meals and food packs for Leicester's self-catered residents."

What is your project about?

Our project works together with a local charity called The Zinthiya Trust to change the lives of those from disadvantaged backgrounds.

The project is a social enterprise which tackes key issues present in Leicester and across the world such as unemployment, homelessness and food waste.

To tackle these issues, Fresh Start's project models involves employing individuals from disadvantaged backgrounds to utilise food waste in order to create soups and smoothies; these can then be sold in shops around Leicester to generate an income for the employees and provide further training in areas such as marketing, sales and finance.

As we are a non-for-profit organisation, the generated revenue will also allow us to run the sale of soups and smoothies on a buy-one-donate-one basis, so for every soup or smoothie bought, we will donate one for free to those in need.

What does your role as project leader involve?

On a day to day basis I am responsible for managing the project and providing direction to the various project members.

I also help bridge the gap between the project members and the executive committee by providing project updates and discussing any arisen issues.

Additionally, it is my responsibility to communitcate and regualrly update our project partner on the project's progress and set dates for focus groups and training.

Furthermore, my role includes applying of various funding opportunities such as Individual Topic Competitions ran by companies such as KPMG and TESCO.

Why did you choose this project?

I chose Fresh Start as it allows me to develop a wide variety of skills such as team work, communication, leadership and organisation.

However, not only am I gaining valuable transferable skills, I am also helping my community and working with a team of dedicated individuals.

Being a part of Fresh Start allows you to have direct contact with the homeless and others in need, as well as local businesses and the sponsor companies which allows you to develop confidence, adaptability and commerial awareness.

  • Email: freshstart@enactusleicester.co.uk

Project Body

"Teaching young children the importance of healthy living and creating an understanding tow."

What is your project about?

To raise awareness about diabetes and heart disease in primary school children to combat the rise in obesity and other related conditions, especially type II diabetes. Through the medium of workshops and school sessions, we aim to teach young children the importance of healthy living and create an understanding tow.

What does your role as project leader involve?

To prepare for weekly meetings, allocating members tasks to aid in project progress and guide and reassure project members towards the project goals.

Why did you choose this project?

The classroom experience is valuable for those who are interested in teaching as well as for those who have a medical background or passionate about healthy living!

Farmers Market

"Improving student nutrition and encouraging students to support local businesses."

What is your project about?

The project aims to improve student nutrition and encourage students to support local businesses. Hosting 2-4 markets a year, the long-running Farmers’ Market project is also one of Enactus Leicester’s commercial project. Furthermore, for the past 12 months, the Farmers’ Market has initiated and supported the growth of another commercial project, now formally known as Revive smoothies.

What does your role as project leader involve?

Organisation and implementation of well-thought out event planning and preparation. As the project leader, I am the main point of contact between all stakeholders and team members within the project. Furthermore, I actively encourage my team to organise aspects of the market in preparation for the event itself.

Why did you choose this project?

I have always been brought up on the importance of a good diet and supporting local produce. As an entrepreneurial individual, I also enjoyed communicating with active businesses and providing a platform for students to experience something a bit different on campus.

  • Project Leader: Alex Marsh
  • Email: enactusleicestermarket@gmail.com

Revive Smoothies

"Creating healthy and flavoured Smoothies in Leicester."

What is your project about?

Establishing a long term commercial project that will produce and sell a smoothie product in Leicester. The smoothies will be as healthy as possible but also be full of flavour. We aim to establish production, selling and overall logistics of the product.

What does your role as project leader involve?

So far my role has involved developing the flavours, logo designs and logistics of distribution and production alongside my fellow team member. I am also in charge of setting and attaining targets in order to achieve our goal of having a finished and distributed product within the foreseeable future.

Why did you choose this project?

I joined Enactus in the early academic year and found the idea of establishing a physical product from scratch extremely enticing. I study management and would like to do something like this in the future so it is the ability to gain experience whilst also helping a great cause.
